2026 Competition Season: Ready, Set, Dance!

EVENTS December 20, 2025 6 min read

The 2026 competition season is almost here, and Gobind Dance Academy is preparing its strongest lineup yet. From Bollywood fusion to traditional Nepali folk, GDA students will be competing across multiple categories at regional and national events.

Competition season is one of the most exciting times of the year at GDA. It is when months of disciplined rehearsal meet the adrenaline of the stage, and when our students discover what they are truly capable of. Whether you are a seasoned competitor or considering your first competition, here is everything you need to know about the upcoming season.

This Year's Categories

GDA students will be competing in four main categories designed to showcase the range of our training. Each category demands a different skill set and artistic approach, giving our dancers the opportunity to stretch beyond their comfort zones.

  • Traditional Folk — Authentic Nepali folk choreography with traditional costume and live-music-inspired timing. Judges look for cultural accuracy, energy, and ensemble cohesion.
  • Bollywood Fusion — Contemporary Bollywood numbers blending classical Indian movement with modern influences. Precision, expression, and stage presence are key.
  • Semi-Classical — Drawing from Kathak and other classical Indian forms, this category emphasizes storytelling through mudra (hand gestures), footwork, and facial expression.
  • Open Choreography — A freer category where GDA's creativity shines. Past routines have blended Nepali folk with hip-hop, contemporary, and even cinematic narrative.

Competition is not about winning a trophy. It is about standing on that stage and knowing that every hour of rehearsal, every correction, every drop of sweat was worth it. The trophy is just a bonus.

— Gobind Rai
GDA dancers at a regional competition in 2025.

Training Camp Schedule

Preparation for competition season begins in January with our intensive training camp. Running every Saturday from 10 AM to 4 PM at the New York studio, the camp covers technique refinement, routine mastery, costume fitting, and stagecraft. Dancers also participate in mock competitions to build confidence and receive feedback from guest judges.

This year, we have invited guest choreographers from both Nepal and the US Bollywood scene to lead masterclasses. These sessions give our students exposure to different teaching styles and artistic perspectives, enriching their overall training experience.

How to Get Involved

Registration for the 2026 competition season is now open. All GDA students enrolled in a regular class are eligible to audition for the competition team. No prior competition experience is necessary — only dedication, discipline, and a passion for dance.

Interested students should speak with their instructor or contact the studio to schedule a placement assessment. Competition team rehearsals begin in January, with the first competition taking place in March.
