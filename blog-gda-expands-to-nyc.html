GDA Expands to New York City

COMMUNITY January 10, 2026 4 min read

After decades of bringing Nepali dance to stages across the United States, Gobind Dance Academy has opened its flagship studio in Elmhurst, Queens — New York City. This expansion marks a new chapter in GDA's mission to preserve and share Nepali dance heritage with the world.

The new studio at 40-08 76th Street is more than just a dance space. It is a cultural hub where the Nepali diaspora in New York can connect with their roots, where New Yorkers of all backgrounds can experience the richness of Nepali movement traditions, and where the next generation of dancers will train.

Why New York City?

New York is home to one of the largest Nepali diaspora communities in the United States. From Jackson Heights to Elmhurst, Queens has become a vibrant center of Nepali culture, with restaurants, grocery stores, and community organizations serving a growing population. Yet until now, there was no dedicated studio offering professional Nepali dance education in the city.

Gobind Rai recognized this gap during his years of travel between Kathmandu and New York for performances and film projects. The demand was clear: families wanted their children to learn the dances of their heritage, adults wanted to stay connected to their culture through movement, and the broader New York audience was eager to discover Nepali dance.

When I first came to New York, I saw how hungry the community was for authentic Nepali dance education. Parents would drive hours to bring their children to weekend workshops. I knew we needed a permanent home.

— Gobind Rai

The Elmhurst location was chosen deliberately. Situated near the 7 train and surrounded by a thriving South Asian community, the studio is accessible to students from all five boroughs as well as neighboring New Jersey and Connecticut.

GDA's New York community celebration at the new studio.

What the New Studio Offers

The New York studio offers the full GDA curriculum, including Traditional Nepali Folk, Bollywood Choreography, Semi-Classical Foundations, and Private Lessons. The space features a sprung dance floor, full-length mirrors, a sound system, and a welcoming lobby for families and visitors.

Beyond regular classes, the studio hosts monthly cultural events, film choreography workshops, and competition training sessions. GDA has already partnered with local Nepali community organizations to produce cultural showcases that celebrate Dashain, Tihar, and other festivals throughout the year.

Looking Ahead

The New York expansion is just the beginning. GDA plans to establish additional locations in other US cities with significant Nepali populations, including Dallas, Washington DC, and Seattle. Each studio will maintain the same commitment to authenticity and excellence that has defined GDA since its founding.

For now, the Elmhurst studio is the beating heart of Nepali dance in New York City. We invite you to visit, take a class, and be part of this growing community.
